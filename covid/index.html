Cancellations abound as 2nd NY case spawns new fears

Local News

Updated: March 2, 9 p.m.



Things are pretty tense in New York right now. The 2nd victim, a Westchester County man in his 50s, is not believed to have contracted the illness from travel, meaning he got it within the United States. The man is in critical condition. Westchester schools closed as a result. (http://bit.ly/2VDumgl). Meanwhile, state officials essentially quarantined a synagogue after a man fell ill during a service.  (http://bit.ly/2PLHRGT). In New York City, work stoppages overseas are starting to affect the bottom line of merchants (http://bit.ly/2IdyzPX). 

Pennsylvania remains unable to conduct COVID-19 testing on their own, but expects to have approval by Friday (http://bit.ly/32OI5lY). Religious officials are being cautious amid the fervor over the virus.  (https://cbsloc.al/2Ijw7ap) 

In New Jersey, Gov. Phil Murphy is urging parents to make contingency plans for things like childcare, should local schools or daycare centers need to close (https://cbsloc.al/2TyYMOa) 

In Connecticut, the governor is waffling on asking the legislature for aid, opting instead to wait out the feds (http://bit.ly/38jKbvo)
