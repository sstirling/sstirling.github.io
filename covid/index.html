Cases double in NY, NJ as crisis deepens nationally

You can feel it now right? It’s still subtle — do I go in for a hug/handshake? Do I stay home from work with this cold? But bit by bit, the coronavirus is chipping away at our daily lives. It’s the beginning of what I expect will be a truly bizarre few months for all of us, so buckle in.

Cases mushroomed again across the region. There are now 41 cases of COVID-19 in the greater New York City metro area, most of which remain in New York (https://nyti.ms/3cFpYDm). But South Jersey and Pennsylvania got their first cases today, as did Rockland County in New York.

Congrats, Connecticut. You have won four-state Survivor, coming soon to a streaming network near you.

But remember: There is a severe testing shortage and only about 2,000 people have been tested nationwide. Odds are, all of these numbers will jump dramatically when this is fixed. (http://bit.ly/2xkJLIp)

Local News

Well I guess we have to start in New York, who continue to lead the charge into this unexpected journey. New York City cases jumped to 16, and Mayor di Blasio pleaded with federal officials for more test kits, which remain in short supply nationwide.  (https://nyti.ms/2VRyaL5).  There appears to be particular concern for the city’s teachers, several of whom are in self-imposed isolation due to symptoms of coronavirus. (http://bit.ly/39x8p6v). Nearly 3,000 people are in self-imposed isolation in New York City alone. Finally, what’s the over/under on this robot surviving the weekend? (http://bit.ly/2VYcKf0) 

 

In New Jersey, the entire K-12 education system is scrambling to figure out how to teach kids at home after the state education department urged them to do so. Can’t express what a monumental challenge this must be (http://bit.ly/32Z1Kzu). Some schools are closing next week just to try and figure it out. (http://bit.ly/3aA87fn) Also, a private high school closed over exposure concerns (http://bit.ly/3aA87fn). Oh, and the virus spread to Camden County, a suburb of Philadelphia. So that’s great. (http://bit.ly/3cFFJds). Legislators were working to pass legislation that mandated Medicaid coverage of coronavirus treatment (http://bit.ly/39uNKAh). 

 

Pennsylvania joined the infected crew, confirming their first two cases, both in the eastern half of the state. (http://bit.ly/38nM3mM). Schools have been proactive, with several shutting down for cleaning (http://bit.ly/3arxboS). The Pennsylvania governor made a disaster declaration. (http://bit.ly/2TDfXOk). 


Connecticut remains without a confirmed case of COVID-19, but the government is warning residents to be prepared for a rough 3 to 4 months. (http://bit.ly/2TIKVou). About 200 are in self-imposed isolation. (http://bit.ly/2VSjSKc).
