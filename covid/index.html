NY moves aggressively to contain outbreak as NJ records first death

It sounds like a knockoff thriller movie, but the "containment zone" is now home to a group of unlucky residents at the center of our region's coronavirus outbreak. 

New York set up a 1-mile radius "containment zone" around the Westchester synagogue where the vast majority of the county's more than 100 cases of COVID-19 originated from. (https://wapo.st/2TCzO1w). It's a serious measure, but less frightening than it sounds. It's primarily geared at discouraging large gatherings — resident movement will not be restricted. 

In New Jersey, the region's first death was recorded.  A horse trainer from Little Ferry, who had a multitude of preexisting conditions, died Tuesday morning after being hospitalized Friday (https://njersy.co/2TNQ3HK). The Record profiled him in 2003. (https://njersy.co/2TQKit0). How we got to here, a timeline of events in New Jersey. (https://njersy.co/3aVCOvV).

Lots of local closings, postponements and changes, as well as other local details, below. 

The prevailing guidance remains: Wash your hands, don't go out if you're sick and heed local authorities.

Local News

Insurers and legislators in the region and nationally have struck deals that should eliminate most of your costs if you need testing or treatment for coronavirus. (http://bit.ly/2W5Qmk3). 

The National Guard will assist efforts to set up a "containment zone" in New Rochelle. (https://nyti.ms/2TEkOjx). Even the graphic looks like it's taking signals from B-apocalypse movies. (http://bit.ly/38Fc8Oj). Confirmed cases jumped to 36 in in New York City, and Mayor Bill de Blasio believes it will continue to accelerate. (https://cnb.cx/2IAsVrl) De Blasio is also encouraging residents to use their sick time — most NYC workers are now entitled to at least 5 days.  New York City jails have a plan to combat the virus (http://bit.ly/2vUXzsB). 

A somber marker as New Jersey records the first death in the region (https://nyti.ms/2IySAAw). Burlington County recorded its first two cases, a married couple who are believed to have contracted the virus in Italy. (http://bit.ly/2WcDN6P). This is the best list I've found of local closures (http://bit.ly/2wNG1yw)

I've also received notices from many of the large colleges and universities in the region, like UCONN in Connecticut, Rutgers, Kean and Seton Hall in New Jersey — that courses are going online, cancelled or spring break is extended. Expect this to continue as schools figure out how to serve their students from a distance. 

Philadelphia confirmed its first case, bringing Pennsylvania's total to 12. City leaders are advising against holding or attending public events. (http://bit.ly/2IAyYMz). Western PA remains largely unaffected to this point, but preparations are ongoing. (http://bit.ly/2Q4bC61). Schools in Pennsylvania are responding more slowly than others in the region, but appear to be moving toward online instruction as well (http://bit.ly/2vKfIJH). 

Connecticut Governor Ned Lamont declared some things giving his administration considerably more authority to act (and change laws) if necessary to combat COVID-19. (http://bit.ly/39GmgYr). A Fairfield County man, the state's first confirmed case, is in a coma. (http://bit.ly/3cQDkwH). The state's second case, in Litchfield County, was also confirmed. (http://bit.ly/2xsMqQe)
